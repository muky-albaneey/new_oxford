# Oxford Manor College Hero Video Script & Production Guide

## Video Concept
A cinematic, inspiring hero video showcasing Oxford Manor College's vibrant campus life, academic excellence, and student community. The video should be 30-60 seconds, loop seamlessly, and convey the school's values of excellence, innovation, and global leadership.

## Video Specifications
- **Duration**: 30-60 seconds (looping)
- **Format**: MP4 (H.264 codec) and WebM (for better compression)
- **Resolution**: 1920x1080 (Full HD) minimum, 4K preferred
- **Aspect Ratio**: 16:9
- **Frame Rate**: 24fps or 30fps
- **File Size**: Optimized for web (under 10MB for 30 seconds)
- **Audio**: None (muted for autoplay compatibility)

## Video Content Structure

### Opening (0-5 seconds)
- **Shot**: Wide aerial or ground-level view of the campus
- **Focus**: Modern school buildings, lush green grounds, architectural beauty
- **Mood**: Grand, inspiring, professional
- **Camera Movement**: Slow push-in or gentle pan

### Middle Section (5-25 seconds)
**Sequence of shots showing student life:**

1. **Academic Excellence** (5-8s)
   - Students in modern science laboratories conducting experiments
   - Close-up of hands working with equipment
   - Students in library studying, collaborating
   - Technology-enabled classrooms with engaged students

2. **Co-Curricular Activities** (8-15s)
   - Sports: Students playing football, basketball, athletics
   - Arts: Drama rehearsal, music performance, art creation
   - STEM: Robotics club, coding activities, science competitions
   - Leadership: Student council meetings, community service

3. **Community & Wellbeing** (15-20s)
   - Students socializing in common areas
   - Teachers mentoring students (one-on-one or small groups)
   - Diverse student body working together
   - Pastoral care moments

4. **Achievement & Celebration** (20-25s)
   - Graduation ceremony or awards
   - Students presenting projects
   - Team celebrations after competitions
   - Confident, smiling students

### Closing (25-30 seconds)
- **Shot**: Return to wide campus view or iconic school building
- **Focus**: School logo or motto visible
- **Mood**: Forward-looking, aspirational
- **Camera Movement**: Slow pull-out or fade

## Visual Style Guidelines

### Color Palette
- Primary: #728639 (Olive green) - use in overlays or branding
- Accent: #d4af37 (Gold) - for highlights, awards, achievements
- Maintain natural colors with slight color grading for premium feel

### Cinematography
- **Lighting**: Natural, bright, optimistic
- **Composition**: Rule of thirds, dynamic angles
- **Movement**: Smooth, professional camera work (dolly, gimbal, or steady cam)
- **Transitions**: Smooth cuts, no jarring transitions
- **Focus**: Shallow depth of field for intimate moments, wide for campus shots

### Mood & Tone
- **Energy**: Uplifting, dynamic but not frantic
- **Emotion**: Pride, achievement, community, aspiration
- **Pace**: Moderate to slow, allowing viewers to absorb the content
- **Quality**: Premium, professional, world-class

## Key Messages to Convey
1. **Excellence**: Academic rigor and outstanding results
2. **Innovation**: Modern facilities and forward-thinking education
3. **Community**: Supportive, inclusive, diverse environment
4. **Global Leadership**: Preparing students for international success
5. **British-Nigerian Excellence**: Unique cultural blend and values

## Production Recommendations

### Equipment Needed
- Professional camera (DSLR/Mirrorless or cinema camera)
- Stabilization (gimbal or steady cam)
- Drone for aerial shots (if permitted)
- Good lighting equipment
- Quality microphones (for any voiceover, though video will be muted)

### Post-Production
- Color grading to match brand colors
- Smooth transitions between shots
- Text overlays (optional, minimal)
- Logo watermark (subtle, bottom corner)
- Optimize for web delivery

2. **Image Carousel**: Rotate through high-quality campus photos
3. **Parallax Effect**: Multiple layers of images with scroll effects

## Current Implementation

The Hero component is already set up to accept video. Simply:
1. Create or obtain the video file
2. Place it at `/public/videos/hero-school.mp4`
3. The component will automatically use it
4. Falls back to image if video fails to load
